A customer walks into a 10,000+ SKU hardware store. They don't want a "1/4 inch zinc-plated hex bolt." They want to "fix a wobbly wooden chair that's kept in a humid basement."

In the physical world, a floor manager translates that intent into the right product. Online, that same customer is met with a search bar that demands technical keywords. If they type their actual problem into a standard Shopify search, they get zero results. This is the **Intent Gap**—the massive disconnect between what a customer actually needs and the rigid, keyword-limited interface of a traditional store.

For high-SKU merchants, this gap is where 30% of your potential revenue disappears.

## The End of the Keyword Era

For years, we’ve trained customers to speak like databases. We forced them to learn our categories and our naming conventions. But as we enter the agentic era, the burden of translation is shifting from the human to the AI.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the 'Intent Gap' in ecommerce?**
The Intent Gap is the disconnect between a customer's actual problem or desire and their ability to find a solution using traditional keyword search. This usually happens in high-SKU stores where products have technical specifications that customers might not know how to search for precisely.

**How do online shopping guides differ from standard search?**
Unlike standard search, which just matches words, agentic online shopping guides use reasoning to understand the context of a query. For example, if you ask for "something for a humid basement," an agentic guide understands that you need rust-resistant items, even if "humid basement" isn't in the product title.

**Why are online shopping guides essential for 10,000+ SKU stores?**
In large catalogs, traditional filters and categories become too complex for users to navigate (the 'Filter Wall'). Online shopping guides simplify discovery by allowing users to ask questions in natural language, instantly surfacing the most relevant products from thousands of possibilities.
