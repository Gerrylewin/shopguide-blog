## Frequently Asked Questions

**What makes a commerce platform "agent-ready"?**
Being agent-ready means your store's data is structured for AI consumption and your checkout process can be executed programmatically. This requires real-time API access to your product catalog, inventory, and pricing, as well as a "Universal Cart" system that allows an agent to build and prepare a checkout link autonomously. Without these, an AI agent is just a chatbot that gives advice but can't close the sale.

**How does ShopGuide's Universal Cart reduce checkout friction?**
Traditional e-commerce requires a user to navigate multiple pages—product, cart, and checkout. ShopGuide's Universal Cart allows the AI Agent to perform these steps in the background. Once a customer expresses intent, the agent builds the cart and provides a direct, pre-populated checkout link. This reduces the journey from minutes of searching to seconds of confirming.

**What is the role of the [Shopify](https://shopify.pxf.io/eK6XxX) Catalog API in Agentic Commerce?**
The Catalog API is the source of truth for an AI Agent. Unlike traditional scraping or feed-based models which can be outdated, the Catalog API provides real-time data on stock levels, variants, and pricing. This ensures that the agent never recommends an out-of-stock item, maintaining customer trust and preventing "broken" shopping experiences.

**Why is human handoff via Slack important for AI agents?**
While AI agents can handle 80-90% of routine transactions, high-value or complex queries still benefit from human expertise. ShopGuide's 1-way Slack integration pings your team when a specific trigger is met (e.g., a high-intent VIP or a request the agent isn't authorized for). This ensures a seamless transition where the human staff has full context of the AI conversation, allowing them to step in and close high-stakes deals instantly.
